# ARCHETYPE 1 FIELD INTERVIEW GUIDE — Inventory Retail

## Purpose
Not a survey. A real conversation, 15-20 minutes, with 8-10 traders (provision stores, boutiques, phone shops — mix a few types). Goal: find out if the researched pain points are actually felt this way in real life, before a line of code is written.

## Opening (build trust, don't pitch yet)
"I'm building something to help shop owners like you keep track of your business better. Can I ask you a few questions about how you currently run things? This isn't a sales pitch — I genuinely want to learn from you."

## Section 1 — Current system
1. How do you currently track your sales and stock? (paper, memory, an app, nothing formal)
2. What happens when a customer buys on credit — how do you remember who owes you?
3. Have you ever lost track of money someone owed you? What happened?
4. How do you know when to reorder something before it runs out?

## Section 2 — Pain validation (test what the research found, don't lead the witness)
5. Has a product ever sold out and you didn't notice until a customer asked for it?
6. Have you ever had stock go missing and you weren't sure if it was theft, a mistake, or you just miscounted?
7. Do you ever feel like the shop is "doing well" but you're still short on cash at the end of the month? (tests the profit-vs-cash finding directly — don't explain the concept, just ask and listen)
8. (Boutique/variant sellers only) How do you track which sizes or colors are selling versus sitting unsold?

## Section 3 — Willingness and trust
9. Have you tried any app or software for this before? What happened — why did you stop, if you did?
10. What would make you trust a new app with your business's money information?
11. If something like this existed and worked well, what would you be willing to pay monthly?

## Section 4 — Close
12. If I built something that did [describe 1-2 things they specifically complained about, in their own words], would you be willing to try it for free for a month and tell me honestly what's wrong with it?

## What to do with the answers
- Write down their exact words, not your interpretation — the real product copy and Guardian alert language should sound like how they actually talk, not how we assume they'd talk
- Flag anything that contradicts the research (e.g. if cash-vs-profit confusion isn't something they recognize, that finding needs revisiting for this specific segment)
- Track how many independently mention the same pain point unprompted — that's the real signal, more than anything they say when asked directly
